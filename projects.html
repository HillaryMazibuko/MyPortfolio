Baseline Mapping – ADDA Emergency Unit (Salima)

This project focused on baseline GIS mapping to support emergency response and development planning activities in Salima District. Baseline mapping involved collecting accurate spatial data to establish an understanding of existing conditions before the implementation of humanitarian or development interventions.

My role included GPS-based field data collection, verification of spatial features, and contributing to the preparation of reliable geospatial datasets. The collected data supported decision-making processes related to emergency preparedness, resource allocation and community-level planning.

SBAPP Project – Land Cover Map Accuracy Assessment (MUST)

This project involved participation in a land cover map accuracy assessment workshop conducted at the Malawi University of Science and Technology (MUST) under the Spatial Biodiversity Assessments, Prioritization and Planning (SBAPP) project. The initiative was implemented in collaboration with the Environmental Affairs Department (EAD) and other key national stakeholders.

The activity contributed to the update of Malawi’s national land cover map, originally released in 2010, which is a critical resource for ecological mapping, biodiversity assessment, and sustainable land use planning. The assessment focused on evaluating the accuracy and reliability of newly generated land cover maps.

During the workshop, I participated in the evaluation of land cover classifications generated using Google Earth Engine, working alongside experts from the Departments of Surveys, Forestry, Land Resources, Physical Planning and the Malawi Environmental Protection Authority (MEPA). The exercise strengthened skills in spatial validation, accuracy assessment, collaborative GIS workflows, and the use of cloud-based geospatial analysis tools.

Dumpsite Suitability GIS Model – Lilongwe City

This project was a GIS-based analytical study aimed at identifying suitable locations for waste disposal sites within Lilongwe City. The objective was to support environmentally sound and socially acceptable waste management planning.

A multi-criteria decision analysis approach was applied using spatial data such as land use, slope, proximity to settlements, hydrology and infrastructure. The output included suitability maps and planning recommendations that can support informed decision-making by urban planners and environmental authorities.

Industrial Waste Management – Project

This project was an academic research and reporting exercise focused on understanding how waste generated from industrial activities can be effectively managed to reduce environmental pollution and public health risks.

The study examined different categories of industrial waste, including solid, liquid, and hazardous waste, and reviewed appropriate handling, treatment and disposal methods. Emphasis was placed on sustainable waste management practices, pollution prevention, and compliance with environmental regulations.

The project enhanced skills in environmental analysis, technical writing and sustainable planning, while providing a strong theoretical foundation in industrial waste management principles.

Land Survey Fieldwork – Golomoti, Mangochi

This project involved hands-on land survey fieldwork conducted in Golomoti, Mangochi, focusing on cadastral and boundary surveys for land administration purposes. The work aimed to support accurate land parcel definition and improve land management practices.

Activities included parcel measurements, boundary demarcation, GPS data capture and ground truthing to ensure spatial accuracy. The fieldwork strengthened practical surveying skills and provided real-world experience in collecting and validating spatial data for legal and planning use.

Personal GIS & Portfolio Website

This project involved the design and development of a personal portfolio website to professionally present my GIS, land surveying and development work. The website serves as a digital platform for showcasing projects, skills and field experience.

The site was developed using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React concepts, with emphasis on responsive design, clear navigation and structured presentation of technical content. This project strengthened my web development skills and ability to integrate geospatial work with modern digital platforms.
